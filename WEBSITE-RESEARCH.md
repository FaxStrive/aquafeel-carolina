## 2. Services Offered

### Free In-Home Water Test
- URL: https://aquafeelsolutions.com/free-water-test/
- Typical market rate $175–$250, offered free
- Technician comes to the home at a convenient time
- Three-stage test protocol:
  1. **TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) test** — measures inorganic and organic chemicals including lead, arsenic, chromium, and other harmful substances
  2. **Chlorine test** — detects chlorine levels (linked to cancer risk and skin problems)
  3. **Water hardness test** — measures calcium/magnesium levels that cause dry skin, hair loss, appliance damage, faded clothes, stained fixtures
- After testing: technician explains contaminants found and presents purification options
- Alternative: "Aquafeel Kit" — at-home test with Zoom consultation with a water safety specialist

### System Installation
- Whole-house system installation included with purchase
- Systems are self-cleaning and low-maintenance
- Computerized controlled valve handles automatic maintenance cycles every two weeks

### Maintenance / Service
- Recommended every 2–3 years (based on usage)
- Technician replaces filters, reprograms system
- NOT covered by warranty — separate cost (~$390 based on customer reports)
- NOTE: This is a pain point — multiple customer complaints about maintenance costs and technician availability

### Organic Products Program
- Three years of complimentary organic household products included with system
- Organic cleaning products
- Organic personal care products
- Ongoing organic products supplied for the lifetime of the equipment
- Claimed 50%+ savings on cleaning supplies

## 10. Industry Notes

### Seasonality
- **Spring (March–May):** High season for water testing — homeowners doing spring cleaning, discovering scaling, stains. CTA: "Schedule your free spring water test."
- **Summer (June–August):** HVAC season — hard water affects cooling systems. Also peak home purchase season (new homeowners discovering water issues). CTA: "Protect your new home's appliances."
- **Fall (September–November):** Pre-winter appliance check. Homeowners want systems installed before holiday hosting.
- **Winter (December–February):** Slower, but skin dryness issues peak (cold air + chlorine water). Health angle is strongest here.

### NC-Specific Water Issues
- **Raleigh area:** Hard water is the #1 issue. Calcium and magnesium concentrations are elevated. Chloramines used for disinfection (instead of chlorine) — harder to remove than chlorine.
- **Charlotte:** Softer water (32 ppm hardness) but PFAS detected. Health angle more important than softening.
- **PFAS contamination:** NC has serious PFAS issues from industrial activity. Strong fear-based marketing opportunity.
- **Well water (rural NC/SC):** Iron, sulfur, sediment, bacteria are major problems. Piedmont and mountain regions have harder groundwater.
- **Lead:** Older homes (pre-1986) may have lead pipes or solder. Strong concern in Durham, older Charlotte neighborhoods.
- **Chloramine:** Raleigh Water Authority uses chloramines — requires carbon block filtration (not just standard carbon). This is a differentiator opportunity.

### Consumer Trends
- Growing awareness of PFAS/"forever chemicals" following national news coverage
- Increased interest in alkaline water driven by wellness culture
- Bottled water fatigue — plastic waste guilt + cost ($1,200+/year per household average)
- Post-COVID heightened health sensitivity — water quality more top-of-mind
- Smart home / connected devices growing — computerized valve is aligned with this trend

### Industry Pricing Context
- Whole-house systems: $3,000–$12,000 installed (Aquafeel at $7,990 is mid-to-high range)
- Water softeners alone: $800–$2,500 (lower entry point for competitors)
- RO systems alone: $300–$800 DIY; $1,500–$3,000 professionally installed
- Aquafeel's bundled pricing (system + organic products + installation + testing) justifies premium vs. component pricing
- Financing (0-down, 90-day deferred) is a strong counter to sticker shock

### Regulatory Tailwinds
- EPA PFAS regulations becoming stricter — more homeowners will be looking for solutions
